Disclaimer

There is a lot of health and fitness information out there, sometimes it can be contradictory, leading to confusion and feeling overwhelmed. Who should you listen to? I've broken down the main sources of information into four groups, these are,

Government Based Health Care

Who

  • Doctors
  • Healthcare Nutritionists
  • Bureaucrats
  • Personal Trainers

Advantages

I want to say this information is normally fairly safe though the spiralling obesity rates gives me reservations. There is obviously something wrong with current policies but there are many that spot on, e.g. having lots of fruit and veg.

Disadvantages

  • Susceptible to inertia meaning new developments in science take a long time to be passed onto the general public.
  • Industrial Pressure: Government policies are subject to economic and industrial pressures, which may not be aligned with your personal interests e.g. it is in the interest of soft drink companies that the goverment supports the studies on the impact of soft drinks on obesity, these companies have a lot of influence on goverment policies through lobbyists.

Athletic community

Who

  • Physios
  • Sports coaches
  • Professional and amateur athletes
  • Gym rats

Advantages

  • Empirical Evidence: Athletes tend to live longer and have a higher quality of life (cite this). Tried and tested and performance based.
  • Low bias, most people are genuinely trying to help and no vested interest.

Disadvantages

  • Pseudo or bro science. (Mostly attributable to gym rats)
  • Most people are not aiming to be professional athletes so this information is not necessarily tailored to them.
  • Performance enhancement may not necessarily translate to a healthy lifestyle, e.g. performance enhancement drugs (PEDs) such as steroids and to a lesser extent synthetic proteins and supplements.

Academic community

Who

  • Scientific journals
  • Academic lectures
  • Journalists

Advantages

  • Scientific method
  • Low bias

Disadvantages

  • Difficult to understand, not approachable.
  • Difficulties associated with clinical studes.
  • Conflicted studies.
  • Industry funded studies may increase bias.

Well being "experts"

Who

  • Vegans
  • Yoga instructors
  • Raw veg enthusiasts
  • Hipsters

As a proud canivore this is really not my area of expertise other than yoga which has evidence of many health benefits. If you're interested in this sort of thing I suggests looking for other sources.

Summary

So who should you listen to? There are many factors but as a general rule I would order them by as the following,

  • Academic Community
  • Athletic Community
  • Government Health

Ultimately it's up to you what you want to believe. Hypotheses in Biological sciences are notorious for being difficult to confirm, for example, keeping children bed ridden and feeding them lard sticks is not exactly an ethical experiment to test the effects of lack of exercise and lard filled diets on children!

But that's not to say that we can't be sure of anything! What is evidently obvious is the fact that a healthy diet high in fruits, vegetables and unprocessed foods as well as regular exercise contribute to a better quality of life and a longer one too.

With that in mind, throughout this site I will endeavor to support my opinions with either,

  • references to clinical studies
  • reference to sources of community opinion
  • explain the basis for my opinion

As a general piece of advice on all information from the internet is to keep a healthy level of cynicism, there are no shortcuts or quick fixes and if something sounds too good to be true it almost definitely is. Also remember each person is unique, what works for one person may not work for you, find what works for you.

About Me

Hi my name is David and I put together this website in order to share some of my experiences and knowledge I've gained in the course of getting fit and healthy. It's almost been two years since I made the decision to be a healthier person, I started off at a weight of 102kg (16 stones and 9 lbs) and a body mass index (BMI) of over 33 (a BMI greater than 31 is clinically obese) and ending up with a weight of 76 kg (just under 12 stones) and a BMI of under 25. The weight loss is just the tip of the iceberg, the change in lifestyle has improved nearly all aspects of life, I sleep better, have improved mood, cognitive ability and most importantly am happier and healthier.
